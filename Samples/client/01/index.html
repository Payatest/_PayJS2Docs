Concepts

PaymentsJS exposes a set of modules that each manage a single piece of functionality. There's three types of module:

  • "Methods" are ways of running a payment -- eg, CreditCard or ACH.
  • "Operations" are things you can do with a method -- eg, Payment or Vault.
  • "Features" are optional extras -- eg, Billing or Recurring.

You can think of methods as nouns and operations as verbs; you create requests by composing phrases like "credit card payment" or "vault ach".

Client-side, a typical PaymentsJS flow goes something like this:

  1. Configure some modules: 1+ methods, 1+ operations, and 0+ features.
  2. Pass the modules to Request.Send() or UI.Render().
  3. Handle the response with a callback function.
